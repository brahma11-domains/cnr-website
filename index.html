Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best country for study abroad?

It depends on your goals, budget, and academic profile. UK is best for quick 1-year masters & post-study work. USA offers STEM OPT & higher salaries. Canada provides PR pathways. Australia balances quality & cost. We help you choose based on your profile.

How much does it cost to study abroad?

UK: ¬£15,000-25,000/year (fees) + ¬£10,000-15,000 (living)
USA: $20,000-50,000/year
Canada: CAD 15,000-25,000/year
Australia: AUD 20,000-35,000/year
We help you find scholarships & education loans to reduce costs.

What is your visa success rate?

Our visa success rate is 98%. We provide expert guidance on financial documentation, interview preparation, and compliance to ensure your visa gets approved. Only rare cases with legal issues face rejections.

Is MBBS abroad valid in India?

Yes! All MBBS programs we recommend are NMC & WHO approved. Graduates can practice in India after passing NEXT exam, and also practice abroad (USA, Canada, UK, etc.) after local licensing.

How long does the application process take?

Typical timeline: 2-3 months (counseling ‚Üí shortlisting ‚Üí applications) + 1-2 months (visa processing) = 4-5 months total. We expedite this where possible.

Do you guarantee admission?

We guarantee 100% admission to at least one university on your shortlist. For MBBS, we have direct admission partnerships with leading universities globally.

The typical documents required include:

  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • Passport copies
  • English proficiency test scores (IELTS/TOEFL)
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation
  • Portfolio (for design/arts programs)
  • Financial documents

Eligibility criteria vary by country and university:

  • Undergraduate: 10+2 with minimum 50-60% marks
  • Postgraduate: Bachelor's degree with 50-60% marks
  • English Proficiency: IELTS 6.0+ or TOEFL 80+
  • Work Experience: Required for some MBA programs

Costs vary significantly by country and university:

  • UK: ¬£12,000-¬£30,000 per year
  • USA: $20,000-$50,000 per year
  • Canada: CAD 15,000-CAD 35,000 per year
  • Australia: AUD 20,000-AUD 45,000 per year
  • Europe: ‚Ç¨5,000-‚Ç¨15,000 per year (many countries offer free education)

Scholarships and part-time work opportunities can help reduce costs significantly.

Visa requirements typically include:

  • Valid passport
  • University offer letter
  • Proof of funds (tuition + living expenses)
  • Health insurance
  • English proficiency proof
  • Medical examination report
  • Visa application fee

Yes! Most countries allow international students to work part-time:

  • UK: Up to 20 hours/week during term, full-time during holidays
  • USA: On-campus work (20 hours/week), OPT after graduation
  • Canada: Up to 20 hours/week during term, full-time during holidays
  • Australia: Up to 40 hours/fortnight during term, unlimited during holidays
  • Europe: Varies by country, typically 10-20 hours/week

Various scholarship options are available:

  • Merit-based: For academic excellence
  • Need-based: For students with financial need
  • Country-specific: Government scholarships (Chevening, DAAD, etc.)
  • University-specific: Institutional scholarships
  • Subject-specific: For particular fields of study

Our team helps identify and apply for suitable scholarships based on your profile.
